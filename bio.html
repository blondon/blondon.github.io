Ben London's Research Website

Before becoming interested in computer science, I studied music and sound engineering at NYU, graduating in 2001. I worked as a sound engineer in New York for a number of years, but eventually returned to school to study computer science. In 2008, I received a master of science in computer science from Columbia University, where I was advised by Tony Jebara. I then worked for several years as a software engineer in the DC metro area before returning to grad school for my PhD. I completed my PhD at the University of Maryland, College Park, where I was advised by Lise Getoor, and was a member of her LINQS lab. In 2015, I started working at Amazon, Seattle, as a Machine Learning Scientist on the Core Machine Learning team. In 2018, I joined the Amazon Music ML team, where I work on (among other things) music recommendation.
