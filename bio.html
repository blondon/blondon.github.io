Ben London Research

Before becoming interested in computer science, I studied music and sound engineering at NYU, graduating magna cum laude in 2001. I worked as a sound engineer in New York for a number of years, but eventually returned to school to study computer science. I took core undergraduate classes at City College, and soon enrolled in a graduate program at Columbia University, where I was advised by Tony Jebara. In 2008, I received a master of science in computer science from Columbia. I then worked for several years as a software engineer in the DC metro area before returning to grad school to complete my PhD. I recently completed my PhD at the University of Maryland, College Park. I was advised by Lise Getoor, and was a member of her LINQS lab. I have also worked closely with Bert Huang and Ben Taskar. This fall, I will start a position at Amazon, Seattle, as a Machine Learning Scientist on their Core Machine Learning team.
